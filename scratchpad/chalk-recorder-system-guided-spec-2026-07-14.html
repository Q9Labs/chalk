Background

Chalk already carries live audio and video through Cloudflare. Recording adds a harder promise: the result must survive a closed browser, a dead worker, delayed processing, and retries without losing ownership or charging twice.

The design separates live capture from later composition. A small native worker saves short encrypted pieces during the meeting. A graphics worker assembles the accepted stage view afterward, when expensive graphics machines can turn on only for real work.

Cloudflare moves live media. Private storage holds bytes. Workers perform bounded jobs. Chalk's control service and durable database decide what exists.

One recording journey

1

Reserve before relying on recording

Scheduled work prewarms. A start-now meeting waits up to 120 seconds for capture to acknowledge.

2

Prove the machine and job

A short-lived certificate proves the worker role; a narrower lease authorizes one fenced attempt.

3

Join Cloudflare and select tracks

The worker receives audible audio, screen share, the active speaker, and low-quality strip participants.

4

Close encrypted bundles

Every 10–15 seconds, media and the decisions that produced the stage become one immutable piece.

5

Replay, compose, and verify

The graphics worker follows the recorded timeline instead of guessing the layout again.

6

Commit the result once

One database transaction makes the video and every transcription chunk job permanent together.

7

Delete temporary authority and media

Cleanup removes bounded inputs and wrapped keys; reconciliation finds anything left behind.

Five rules connect every implementation lane

1 · Identity

Who is speaking?

A current machine and role, proven by short-lived credentials.

 2 · Assignment

What may it do?

One immutable job, attempt, lease, deadline, and fence.

 3 · Storage + keys

Which bytes may move?

The server chooses every object key, operation, condition, size, and expiry.

 4 · Progress

Is this attempt current?

Heartbeats, progress, failures, and stale reports share one vocabulary.

 5 · Finalization

When does the result exist?

Only one transaction may commit the video and transcript handoff.
